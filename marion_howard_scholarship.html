Marion D. Howard Nursing Scholarship

Marion Howard, an Auburn resident for over 50 years, initially pursued her degree at the Heywood Memorial Hospital School of Nursing in Gardner, before receiving Master's degrees at Anna Maria University and Boston College. Her educational journey continued with the pursuit of advanced degrees, highlighting her commitment to the profession. Beyond being a dedicated practitioner, Marion played a pivotal role in not only caring for her patients but also in the leadership of numerous programs and clinics, most notably at St. Vincent’s Hospital. Additionally, she took pride and great joy in providing mentorship to aspiring nursing students as a Nursing Educator. Post-retirement she continued her love for the field, working as a parish nurse, a substitute nurse in the Auburn and Leicester schools, and a member of the Auburn Board of Health. Throughout her career and in her daily life, Marion embodied qualities of selflessness, consistently dedicating her time and talent to those in her care and within her community. Applicants for this award are encouraged to mirror Marion’s exemplary characteristics through a short 250-word essay. The award committee will prioritize students who exhibit a strong passion for becoming a nurse, a commitment to advancing the nursing profession, and a sincere dedication to compassionately caring for others. Please prepare an essay that highlights your demonstrated qualities throughout high school and share your inspiration for wanting to pursue this field. The Marion D. Howard Scholarship Committee will review applications and award one student a non-renewable $500 award.
